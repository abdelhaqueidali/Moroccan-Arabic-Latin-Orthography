# Moroccan Darija Latin Orthography (Quick Reference)

A standardized Latin orthography for Moroccan Arabic (Darija), adapted from the **Standard Amazigh Latin alphabet**. It replaces Arabizi with a consistent, keyboard-friendly writing system suitable for everyday writing, education, and digital processing.

## Alphabet

| Sound | Letter | Replaces |
|-------|--------|----------|
| ع | **ɛ** | 3, ʿ |
| ش | **c** | sh, ch |
| غ | **ɣ** | gh, ġ |
| خ | **x** | kh, ḫ |
| ح | **ḥ** | ħ |
| ض | **ḍ** |  |
| ص | **ṣ** |  |
| ط | **ṭ** |  |

- **o** is omitted (write **u** instead).
- **p** and **v** are reserved for recent loanwords.
- Foreign names may keep their original spelling (*Paris*, *Python*).

## Hamza (ء)

- Written as **e**.
- Use **ê** after a consonant when needed to distinguish it from schwa.
- Word-initial hamza is usually omitted:
  - *insan*, *limtiḥan*
- Optional **ʼ** may be used for phonetic or Standard Arabic distinction:
  - *ʼinsan*, *lʼinsan*

## Definite Article

- Always attached:
  - *lbab*
  - *lqelb*
  - *lḥal*

Sun letters assimilate:

- *ssuq*
- *ccariɛ*
- *ṭṭriq*

With foreign words:

- *l'visa*
- *l'podcast*

## Aspect & Future

Written as separate words.

- *ka ncuf*
- *ka nekteb*
- *ɣa nji*
- *ɣadi nji*

## Negation

Pattern:

> **ma ... -c**

Examples:

- *ma bɣit-c*
- *ma ka ncuf-c*
- *ma ndir-c*

## Prepositions

Separate before nouns:

- *f ddar*
- *b ssuq*

Hyphenated with pronouns:

- *fi-ya*
- *li-k* / *l-ek*
- *fi-k* / *f-ek*
- *mɛa-na*

## Attached Pronouns

Hyphenated:

- *qelb-i*
- *jab-ha*
- *ɛṭa-ni*

## Demonstratives

Hyphenated:

- *had-a*
- *had-i*
- *had-uk*

Contracted forms are fused:

- *dak*
- *dik*
- *duk*

## Schwa (e)

Write **e** where it is naturally pronounced.

Examples:

- *lemdina*
- *lekbir*
- *nɛes → ka tenɛes*
- *nsa → ka nensa*

## Nouns

Masculine:

- *rajel*
- *bab*

Feminine:

- *mdina*
- *xedma*

When suffixes are added:

- *mdina → mdint Zagura*
- *xedma → xedmt-i*

## Writing Principles

- One letter per phoneme where possible.
- Avoid digraphs (*sh*, *gh*, *kh*).
- Use existing Latin keyboard characters.
- Hyphenate attached grammatical elements for clarity.
- Preserve original spelling of non-integrated foreign words.
- Focus on orthographic consistency, not lexical standardization.
